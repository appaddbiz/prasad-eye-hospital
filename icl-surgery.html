ICL Surgery

Introduction

For individuals seeking an alternative to LASIK surgery, Implantable Collamer Lens (ICL) Surgery provides a safe and effective solution for permanent vision correction. ICL surgery is particularly beneficial for those with high myopia, thin corneas, or dry eyes who may not be ideal candidates for traditional laser eye surgery.

What is ICL Surgery?

ICL Surgery involves the implantation of a biocompatible lens inside the eye to correct vision without removing corneal tissue. The Implantable Collamer Lens (ICL) is placed between the natural lens and the iris, effectively treating refractive errors such as myopia (nearsightedness), hyperopia (farsightedness), and astigmatism.

Unlike LASIK or PRK, which reshape the cornea, ICL Surgery provides permanent vision correction without altering the corneal structure, making it a reversible and customizable procedure.
